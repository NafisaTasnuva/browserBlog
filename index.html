The Honeycomb Shelter

My angle

This project is for our Maker lab class 1 where my team and I were required to design something that would be able to address a social issue of our community. Knowing that winter was approaching, we decided that it would be best to work on a shelter that is portable, transportable, and durable to withstand harsh weather conditions for people who are experiencing homelessness. We are not required to construct a physical prototype due to covid restrictions and so our project will be aided by digital prototyped and sketching. Though this concept may be difficult for us to create in-person with our limited access to the needed materials we are hoping that the freedom to design digitally will allow us the opportunity to proceed. . Our concept incorporates the idea of the DIY culture which includes the shared act of construction, joint conversation, and reflection. (Ratto 253) We were inspired by an origami, so the shelter can be easy to pop up and collapse, as well as allowing it to be portable. It was important to keep in mind that the making of this product has to be shareable and accessible so no one is excluded in being able to set up our shelter (Davies 90).

sources:
